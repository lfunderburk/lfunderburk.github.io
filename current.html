Synaesthesia Creates

I am currently taking online courses on HTML, HTML5, CSS, CSS3, JavaScript,PHP and MySQL as my goal is to build an interactive website for synaesthetes to connect and share their gifts.

As synaesthetes, it is common for us to be laughed at or not validated in our experiences. I personally think that celebrating our quirks is what allows us to create beautiful things and I want to contribute in this by providing a space for people to share what makes them unique.

Through this website I will also post relevant information on Workshops whose theme is synaesthesia and the expression of one's Self through music, dancing and painting.

Workshop Facilitation

My goal is to first learn from those who have facilitated workshops on a wide range of topics and also learn the administration details that usually occur behind the scenes. My strategy was to attend workshops on a variety of formats before facilitating. Through the implementation of this strategy I learned the crucial role that self growth plays in workshop facilitation.

Self growth

Since January 2016, I have attended numerous workshops through Sense - a workshop series led by Loretta Laurin, on several occasions I have witnessed and been part of Empathy and Vulnerability - a workshop led by Jonathan and Michael Bean. I enjoy practicing meditation and I have worked one-on-one along with personal coach Jeridan Kowal. During Summer 2016 I attended the MBSR program with Dr. Erika Horowitz and learned the importance of practicing mindfulness in my daily life.

Courses

On Summer 2016, I attended a course on Workshop Design and Facilitation led by Anna Soole and learned to design workshops that practice inclusiveness of gender identities and expressions, that support the deaf and LGBT communities and that acknowledge our ancestors and recognizes Vancouver as unceded Aboriginal territory.

Workshops

I facilitated my first workshop on Synaesthesia during the Winter Intention gathering that took place from December 30 2015 to January 3 2016. During this workshop, I guided participants through meditation and a series of activities to explore the senses of sight and touch, and to express their experience, thoughts and feelings in the form of a painting.


On Summer 2016, I facilitated for the second time during the music festival Re:Unity. During this workshop I led a series of games and discussions around synaesthesia, embracing one's own quirks and expressing emotion through body painting. I was lucky to have found another synaesthete in this workshop and was touched by learning from his own experiences. At the end of the workshop, participants explained the meaning of their body painting.

Past Projects

Resurgence and Re:Unity

Intention is one of the only consensus-based participant driven and volunteer-run conscious local summer gatherings. I joined the Intention Community over a year ago and first stepped in as an organizer for Resurgence in January 2016. My main focus in Resurgence this summer revolved around Workshop Coordination as well as recruiting and leading First Aid volunteers.

During Summer 2016 our team our goal was for the Psytrance and the Intention communities to come together and play, dance and learn in a meeting of the tribes. As organizers, our team faced a number of challenges and Resurgence was eventually cancelled.

After the chaos that cancelling brought, three more orgs and I decided to take the valuable pieces we had left and threw a two-day event in Vancouver with music, workshops and a BBQ fundraiser. Thus the meeting of the tribes took place.

I plan to be an organizer once more for this Winter's Intention Gathering <3

Sense Workshop Series

From January 2016 until May 2016, I worked along with Loretta Laurin, founder of Sense. I started out by saving testimonials from participants throughout the series and then took on the role of Drop-In Administrator.

As a Drop-in Admin, I maintained communication with all workshop facilitators regarding their needs as well as maximum capacities for their workshops. I also made use of a roster and Excel spreadhseets to keep track of registered participants, who could either be Alumni, drop-ins, facilitators and volunteers.

Working on the administrative aspect of running Sense has given me insight into the importance of having excellent written communication skills and also the importance of problem-solving and reasoning that my Mathematics background has to offer.
